# Augmented Reality Changing the E-commerce Reality 
### Evaluating Virtual Try-On as a Strategic Growth Lever for Online Jewellery Retailers
Business Analysis Case Study | Author: Shruti Gupta

## Executive Summary
As online shopping continues to grow, retailers face a fundamental challenge: customers cannot physically experience products before making a purchase. This challenge is particularly significant in the jewellery industry, where buying decisions are highly influenced by appearance and personal confidence.

This case study explores how Augmented Reality-powered Virtual Try-On (VTO) can address these challenges by improving product visualization, enhancing customer confidence, and supporting business growth.

The analysis focuses on:

-Business challenges

-Stakeholder needs

-Solution requirements

-Expected business outcomes

-Success measurement criteria

## Business Objective
To evaluate whether Augmented Reality-powered Virtual Try-On can improve customer confidence, reduce purchase friction, and enhance key e-commerce performance metrics for online jewellery retailers.

## Business Challenge
Online retail has solved convenience. It has not completely solved confidence. While customers can browse thousands of products from anywhere, many still hesitate when making high-consideration purchases such as jewellery. The fundamental challenge remains: How can online retailers recreate the confidence of an in-store shopping experience without a physical store?

## Current State Analysis (As-Is)
### Traditional Online Jewellery Purchase Journey
Customer visits website ------> Browses product -----> Views static images ---->Questions whether the product will suit them ----> Leaves website or postpones purchase

### Key Friction Points
-Style Doubt: "Will this suit me?" leads to purchase hesitation.

-Visual Disconnect: "How will it look on me?" results in lower engagement.

-Design Uncertainty: "I'm not sure about the design" causes cart abandonment.

-Physical Barrier: "I need to see it first" creates lost conversion opportunities.

The gap between product discovery and purchase confidence remains one of the largest barriers in online jewellery retail.

## Future State Design (To-Be)
### Introducing AR-Powered Virtual Try-On Experience
The proposed future state introduces an Augmented Reality-powered Virtual Try-On (VTO) capability directly within the product discovery and evaluation process.

By allowing customers to visualize jewellery on themselves in real time, the solution aims to reduce purchase uncertainty, improve engagement, and support more confident buying decisions.

### Future Customer Journey
Customer visits website ----> Selects jewellery product ----> Launches Virtual Try-On -----> Visualizes product in real time -----> Builds purchase confidence -----> Completes transaction

## Solution Evaluation
The proposed Virtual Try-On solution was evaluated against the key business challenges identified during the current-state analysis.

-Purchase Hesitation: Enables real-time product visualisation before purchase, directly addressing the stage where customers question whether the product will suit them after viewing static images.

-Cart Abandonment: Improves customer confidence during the decision-making process, preventing the final step in the journey where a customer leaves the website or postpones their purchase.

-Low Engagement: Encourages interactive product exploration, providing a dynamic alternative to the traditional step of only viewing static images.

-Limited Product Experience: Replicates essential aspects of in-store evaluation, solving the friction point that causes users to postpone purchases because they cannot visualize the item on themselves.

## Stakeholder Analysis
Successful implementation requires alignment across multiple stakeholder groups.

-Customers: Gain the confidence to overcome suitability doubts after viewing static images.

-Marketing Team: Increase engagement to prevent drop-off during the browsing phase.

-Sales Team: Boost conversions by stopping customers from leaving or postponing their purchase.

-Product Team: Deliver an experience that directly answers the "Will this suit me?" question.

-Technology Team: Deploy scalable alternatives to the static image barrier.

-Leadership: Drive revenue growth by capturing lost conversion opportunities.

## Business Requirements
The proposed solution should support the following capabilities

Functional Requirements:

-FR-01: Users should be able to launch the Virtual Try-On experience using their device camera.

-FR-02: Users should be able to visualise jewellery products in real time.

-FR-03: Users should be able to switch between multiple product variants.

-FR-04: Users should be able to save or capture their experience.

-FR-05: Users should be able to share their try-on experience externally.

## Recommendation
Based on the analysis, Virtual Try-On has the potential to address one of the most significant challenges in online jewellery retail: the inability to experience products before purchase.

By improving visualization and purchase confidence, AR-powered experiences can support stronger customer engagement, higher conversion potential, and enhanced competitive differentiation.

A phased implementation beginning with high-value product categories is recommended to validate impact against predefined business metrics.
